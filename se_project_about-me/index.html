Marine Corps Living

I truly enjoyed my time since the age of 17, being in the Marine Corps. I got out a few years later after having health issues. I still maintain friendships that are ever-lasting. This led me down my path of re-discovering how much I love working with technology.

From TV to Admin

I studied acting for years at New York Film Academy and was able to work on shows such as American Crime. While doing this, I would discover new software programs and learn to tinker with them. My day job after those years would be doing clerical work and auditing financials.

Through My Lense

During film school, I discovered that I enjoyed taking photos and editing them. My family thought that I should create a side Photography business. After film school and during the time I was doing clerical work during the day, I would photograph people and places on the weekends. My side job led me back to programming. I didn't have the funds to pay thousands of dollars or even hundreds to a website creator; so I learned how to build my own website. I had a lot of errors where it would explode but then I figured out that I had to purge the cache every once in a while. Unfortunatley, that website is no longer available. This was another step towards the rediscovery of my first love.

Technology Gives Butterflies

I was 6 years old when I started tinkering in the back of boom boxes; taking apart cassette tapes, and re-rolling them to see how they functioned. My brain has always been that way. At the age of 8 or 9, I learned how to open a backdoor to get free internet for a longer period after the free-trials from the cd-roms found at box stores. Then when MySpace started gaining popularity, I tried it out. It was this fascinating new world that I wanted to break and see how to put it back together again. I quickly learned how to add CSS animations that I would copy online and create moving snow with html that would zig-zag on my page. However, don't ask if I remember what I did back then, it was a very long time ago. This is me, and I love to code!
